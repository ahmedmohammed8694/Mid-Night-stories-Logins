1. What's New in v1.01
v1.0 delivered the core platform: anonymous submissions, moderation queue, comments, likes, sharing, and the safety pipeline. v1.01 is a focused enhancement pass — it does not change the core architecture. It adds:
Full rebrand to “Midnight Stories” with the new tagline, applied site-wide.
Research-driven font pairing and colour palette (dark, whisper-quiet aesthetic).
Dynamic UI/UX: scroll animations, micro-interactions, skeleton loading, dark theme.
A new LGBTQ+ story category on the main feed/results page.
A permanent Support & Resources page with verified US and India helplines.
A companion AI agent prompt (see the accompanying file) that a coding agent can run against the existing codebase to execute this plan.
Scope note
This is additive polish on top of an already-working site. Nothing here should touch or weaken existing moderation, rate-limiting, or reporting logic — treat any conflict as a stop-and-flag situation, not something to silently override.
2. Research Tasks (Do Before Building)
2.1 Competitor & design research
Search and review 5–8 sites in the anonymous story-sharing / peer-support / confession-community space. For each, capture: navigation structure, how sensitive content is presented, visual cues for anonymity, feed/card layout, and category structure. Summarize patterns worth adapting vs. avoiding. Never copy layouts, copy, or art directly — use only as inspiration.
2.2 Typography research
Search current best-practice font pairings for emotionally sensitive, editorial, text-heavy websites. Shortlist 2–3 heading/body pairings, favoring free/open web fonts (e.g. Google Fonts) for cost and licensing simplicity. Confirm readability at small sizes since this is a reading-heavy site.
2.3 Colour scheme research
Search current palette guidance for a nighttime, low-glare, emotionally calm theme fitting “Midnight Stories.” Shortlist 2–3 palettes covering background, surface, primary/secondary accent, and success/error states. Verify WCAG AA contrast for body text and buttons before finalizing.
Deliverable for this section: a one-page research summary (competitor notes + font shortlist + palette shortlist with hex codes and contrast check) reviewed by a human before any code is written.

5. LGBTQ+ Story Category
Add “LGBTQ+” as a first-class filterable category on the feed/results page, alongside existing categories.
Same moderation standard as every other category — no lower or higher bar, no separate weaker/stronger review path.
Visually distinguish with a subtle tag/badge; never expose or imply identity of the anonymous poster.
Update Community Guidelines to explicitly confirm this category is welcome and held to the same guidelines as all others.
6. Support & Resources Page (US + India)
Add a permanent, one-click-reachable “Support & Resources” page (linked from the footer of every page, not gated behind search or login). Suggested starting content — verify all numbers/hours before publishing, and re-verify periodically, since helplines change over time:
United States
988 Suicide & Crisis Lifeline — call or text 988, available 24/7.
The Trevor Project (LGBTQ+ youth) — 1-866-488-7386, 24/7, also available via chat/text.
India
Tele-MANAS — Government of India 24/7 mental health helpline — 14416 or 1-800-891-4416.
Vandrevala Foundation crisis helpline — 24/7 phone/text/chat support — confirm current number before publishing.
LGBTQ+-affirmative option, e.g. The Humsafar Trust or iCall — confirm current contact details before publishing.
Label the page clearly as peer support, not emergency response, with a one-line note to contact local emergency services for an immediate crisis.
7. Rollout Plan
Complete research tasks (Section 2) and get sign-off on font, colour, and competitor findings.

Bump version marker to v1.01 site-wide and publish a CHANGELOG entry summarizing all of the above.
Spot-check accessibility (contrast, reduced motion, screen reader labels, keyboard nav) after every visual change, not just at the end.
